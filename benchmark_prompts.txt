# --- Pedagogical axis (16 questions, 2 per sub-axis) ---

# Simple explanation (novice)
Explain photosynthesis in simple terms to someone who has never heard of it before.
Describe what inflation is to someone who has never studied economics.

# Level adaptation (advanced learner)
Explain recursion to someone who already understands functions and wants to go deeper.
Explain the CAP theorem to someone familiar with basic distributed systems.

# Analogy generation
Give a good analogy to help someone understand how vaccines work.
Find an analogy that explains how a neural network learns.

# Assessment design
Create a 3-question quiz on the water cycle suitable for a secondary-school student.
Create 3 multiple-choice questions that test understanding of supply and demand.

# Misconception handling (learner-delivered)
My friend told me we only use 10% of our brain. Is that actually true?
I read online that the Great Wall of China is the only man-made structure visible from space — is that right?

# Structured simplification (bullet form)
Explain in simple bullet points why the sky appears blue.
List the main reasons in bullet form why the Earth has seasons.

# Student guidance (procedural + troubleshooting)
What are the main steps for writing a research literature review?
I'm struggling with calculus — where should I start?

# Critical thinking / trade-offs
What are the trade-offs between renewable and fossil-fuel energy sources?
What are the main arguments for and against universal basic income?
